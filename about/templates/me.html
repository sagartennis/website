I am from Bangalore, India. It is also called the Silicon Valley of India. This leads to the cross-over of many cultures. I moved to the US when I was 18. Before, this I never traveled alone outside the country. I had no family to help me adjust to the culture. I attended Morgan State University for my undergrad which is an HBCU (Historically Black Colleges and Universities) and Univesity of Oregon for my grad school. The two schools are not only located opposite geographically on the map but also are very different culturally. The two different perspectives I received here shaped my understanding of people from different backgrounds.

The tennis team at Morgan did not have one single American (US) on the team. The team members hailed from Europe, South America, Caribbeans, and Asia. This helped the team as a group because we were a bunch of misfits trying to settle into the new culture and norm that was thrown at us. The three words that describe me best from all my experiences are: Adaptable, Diverse and, Socially aware.

Adaptable

I was able to able to adapt to a new country and culture without any immediate family around and excel in my education and athletics. I graduated with a GPA of 3.7 /4.0. To take up a business degree that is completely different from a technical degree like engineering required quick and drastic change. Even cultures within the US differed between geo-locations. Going from the Eastcoast to the Westcoast was a completely new experience for me. These constant changes made me look at the same problem from a different angle which has helped in my personal growth.

Diverse

I am multi-lingual. The languages known to me are: English, Kannada, Hindi, Marathi. I live in Bangalore, Karnataka which is the IT capital of India. This makes it one of the most diverse places in India. People from all over the country and world come to Bangalore. I attended Morgan State University which is an HBCU (Historically Black Colleges and Universities). The tennis team at Morgan did not have one single US citizen. I attended the University of Oregon for my MS which is cultural very different from Morgan.

Socially Aware

My diverse background and experiences have made me socially aware and situations around the world. It has definitely helped me be open-minded about someone else opinions that aren't equivalent to mine. Staying six years in the US has helped me to understand not only the cultures of America but also through my tennis team I was able to learn about Europe. Staying away from home gave me a new perspective to look at home and its situation.
