I started playing tennis at the age of 4. When I started, I did not know the sport would change my life forever. The sport taught me discipline, the importance of hard work. But above all, it taught me how to face failures. I competed in all age categories at the national level.

Morgan State University.

MEAC (Mid Eastern Athletic Conference)


The Morgan State Bears participates in the NCAA Division I MEAC (Mid Eastern Athletic Conference). As a sport, tennis is one of the most popular sports at the University. The men's tennis at MSU has won 7 consecutive conference titles since 2012.

I was recruited on a scholarship to the team in the year 2013. I was given a great opportunity to continue my passion in the US under the great guidance of the coaching team and staff of Morgan State University. Soon after joining the team, I became an integral part of the team. I was a regular starter for the team playing at singles no.6 and doubles no.2. The team went on to win the conference in 2013 and every year until I graduated.

My senior year was one of the best years in terms of tennis adolescents. The team went on to win the conference again that year (2017). I received two awards that year: All American Scholar and The Most Improved Player.

My overall experience at Morgan State University through tennis was beyond surreal. Going from India to the USA at the age of 18 made me independent and adaptable. I was able to see the world through a different cultural lens. I gained a new perspective on understanding the world.
